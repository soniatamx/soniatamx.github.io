Toronto Star Touch
UX Guidelines

Goals: Create a thorough and organized website that would serve as UX guidelines to the Toronto Star on how to create screens for the tablet app.

My Roles: HTML & CSS, JavaScript, UI/UX design, content writing

Breaking it Down

When I started working on the UX guidelines my goal was to organize the vast amount of information as best I could. Since there were so many components and layers to deal with, the UX director and I thought the best way to organize the content would be to apply atomic design. So, I broke down each UI element we had into smaller pieces that could be combined into components then, built into layers, then templates and finally assembled into our daily editions.

Early Wireframing

The main goal of the experience was to get the designers and writers working with the guidelines effortlessly, whether it was through a linear fashion or going back and forth between different elements. I wanted to ensure users were never lost in the website so I created a highlighting anchored scroll navigation so users knew exactly where they were in the guidelines and could click an anchor and seamlessly scroll to another section of the site.
